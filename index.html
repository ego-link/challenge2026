Benchmarking Socially‑Aware Embodied AI

EgoLink introduces a large‑scale egocentric benchmark that uses multi‑dimensional multiple‑choice questions (MCQs) to evaluate reasoning over emotions, causal logic, and behavioral intents in real‑world human interactions.

Dataset Foundation & Scale

EgoLink transforms the E3 egocentric emotion dataset into a comprehensive social reasoning benchmark.

  • Base Dataset – E3: large‑scale egocentric video corpus for embodied emotion understanding (project page).
  • Video Collection: ~20,000+ egocentric clips covering diverse social scenarios.
  • Total Duration: 70+ hours of annotated egocentric footage.
  • Scenario Coverage: daily conversations, meetings, social gatherings, education, services, family.
  • Participant Diversity: 500+ individuals across demographics, ages, and cultures.
Real‑world egocentric video Emotion‑centric foundation Social interaction focus

Design Philosophy & Splits

From perception to social cognition via a two‑tier supervision strategy.

  • Limited Supervision: 5,000 carefully designed MCQs encourage generalizable reasoning over pattern memorization.
  • Transfer Learning: models leverage E3 emotion annotations and pretrained knowledge to bridge perception and social reasoning.
  • Cognitive Leap: MCQs test causal inference, temporal generalization, intent prediction, and multimodal synthesis beyond basic MER.

Evaluation & Maintenance

Objective, MCQ‑based metrics and long‑term support for the benchmark.

  • Evaluation Protocol: MCQ format minimizes subjective bias and enables reproducible, fine‑grained analysis.
  • Primary Metric: Overall Top‑1 Accuracy across all MCQs.
  • Dimension‑wise Scores: breakdown over emotional perception, social causality, intent prediction, and semantic summarization.
  • Maintenance Commitment: official website, leaderboard, and evaluation infrastructure will be maintained through 2029 (at least three years of guaranteed access).

Ethics & Future Extensions

Privacy‑aware design with a roadmap for richer egocentric social intelligence.

  • Ethical Compliance: explicit consent from video owners; alignment with YouTube, Douyin, and Bilibili data policies; transparent data usage and withdrawal rights.
  • Data Minimization: collection of only necessary information (portrait, voice, etc.) with strict boundaries for research use.

Future extensions will enrich temporal depth, embodied actions, and dialogue structure.

Temporal & Longitudinal

Follow‑up recordings of evolving social relationships; frame‑level annotations to support fine‑grained temporal modeling.

Embodied Action Grounding

Hand gestures, body movements, and physical interactions linking social cognition with visible embodied actions.
